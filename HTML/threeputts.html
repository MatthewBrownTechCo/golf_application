Strokeplay Rules

The rules of Strokeplay are simple. Each time that you hit the golf ball, you accrue a stroke. After all 18 holes have been completed, whoever has hit the ball in all 18 holes in the least amount of total strokes wins!

Each hole at a golf course has a set par. “Par” represents the number of strokes a golfer is expected to make on the hole or course. Many golfers like to tally their +/- on each hole based on that par value. For example, if you are on a par 4 hole, but hit the ball in the hole in 3 strokes, your score on that hole is "-1". Alternatively, if you hit the ball in the hole in 6 strokes, your score is "+2" for that hole.
